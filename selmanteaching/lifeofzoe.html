ZOE LIFE: THE THEOLOGY OF LIFE

John tells us that from a spiritual standpoint there are two kinds of birth. The Bible says you can be born of blood and the will of man, contrasted against being born of God.

Two Kinds of Birth

  • Physical / Natural / Biological Birth
  • Rebirth / Spiritual Birth

Jesus said, “That which is born of flesh is flesh, but that which is born of Spirit is spirit.”

Natural Birth

  • Our will is not part of the equation—we were not given liberty to choose.
  • Requires both man and woman.
  • Requires time (9 months).
  • Has a validity period: the lifetime.

Spiritual Birth

  • Our will decides—we choose to be born again.
  • Happens instantly by the hearing of faith.
  • Does not require man and woman—just God’s Word and Spirit.
  • The official gateway to Zoe life.
“All things were created by Him; without Him was not anything made that was made.” — John 1:3

Three Kinds of Life

  • Bios: Physical life.
  • Psuché: Soulical life (Matthew 15:25).
  • Zoe: The God-kind of life — supernatural, uncreated, indestructible, eternal.

What Does It Mean to Be Born Again?

  1. Born of God — Restored to the Father as the prodigal son was restored.
  2. Born of the Spirit — Receive the characteristics of the Spirit, manifest supernatural qualities like wisdom, kindness, joy.
  3. Born of the Word — The incorruptible seed of God’s Word (1 Peter 1:23).

Three Gifts at Salvation

  • Forgiveness of sin.
  • Gift of righteousness.
  • The life of God (Zoe).

Implications of Having Zoe

  1. Oneness with Christ — Like husband and wife becoming one (Ephesians 2).
  2. Positional Advantage — Elevated above principalities and powers.
  3. Access to His Name — Authority through conscious use of the name of Jesus (Colossians 3:17).

Finished Reality vs Manifest Reality

Realities are finished in Christ (prophetic realities) but must manifest in our lives. The prayer is: “Thy will be done on earth as it is in heaven.” (Matthew 6:10)

“Let us hold fast the profession of our faith without wavering; for He is faithful that promised.” — Hebrews 10:23

The Journey of Zoe

When you receive Christ, it is your spirit that receives salvation. Your soul and body must go through a gradual process called the renewal of the mind. The Spirit of God, partnered with your obedience, determines the pace of this transformation.

The first law of liberty is acknowledging what is finished in Christ, even before manifestation. Faith comes by hearing—even your own declarations build faith.
